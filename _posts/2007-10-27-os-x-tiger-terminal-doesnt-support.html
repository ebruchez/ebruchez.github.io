--- layout: post title: The OS X Tiger terminal doesn't support accents date: '2007-10-27T12:35:00.000-07:00' author: Erik Bruchez tags: - osx - apple - i18n modified_time: '2008-01-04T01:39:34.965-08:00' thumbnail: http://bp2.blogger.com/_DSqG0J8ANmg/RyOfgVJEvII/AAAAAAAAACU/DGzHgBsUrd4/s72-c/terminal.png blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-2849901641571065621.post-8745235683078501785 blogger_orig_url: https://blog.bruchez.name/2007/10/os-x-tiger-terminal-doesnt-support.html --- I was stunned to realize that the Mac OS X Tiger terminal doesn't support accents (and other non-ASCII characters) by default. This includes pasting accented characters in the terminal, listing files with accents in their names, etc.

In fact, this is not really a question of the terminal: it seems that the versions of bash, ls, and other core utilities that come with Tiger are too old and don't support UTF-8. Wow: Tiger came out in 2005. Didn't Java have support for UTF-8 back in 1995 already? This is 2007 and just the kind of things that should work out of the box, right?

Anyway, I had some luck by installing MacPorts and following the suggestions in this post, although this still doesn't work well with iTerm which is unable to combine diacritics.

But maybe I am a little unfair as I am still using Tiger. Please tell me that this is now fixed in Leopard.
